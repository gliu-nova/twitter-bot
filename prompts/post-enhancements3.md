Very good progress, this is close to production quality. I just want to make some small polishing enhancements if possible. 
Do not redesign the system or change the overall WHAT → WHY → WHY IT MATTERS structure.

Instead, improve the writing quality with these principles:

1. Every sentence should contribute new information. Avoid repetitive endings/implications.

Avoid repeating the headline in the body.

Example to avoid:

Headline:
"ETH futures premium widens"

Body:
"The futures premium widened vs spot."

These communicate the same idea.

Instead:

Headline:
"ETH futures premium widens"

Body:
"Futures are trading farther above spot than before."

Each line should move the story forward.

2. Headlines should maximize immediate comprehension.

Keep headlines concise and event-driven.

Prefer:

BTC exchange prices converge
BTC funding turns negative
ETH futures premium widens
BTC open interest reaches a new high

Avoid unnecessarily long headlines unless additional detail adds meaningful value.

3. Prefer natural financial English.

Read every template as if it were written by a human market analyst.

Avoid phrases that sound generated or awkward.

Examples:

Instead of:
"Funding reflects a lean toward long exposure."

Prefer:
"Traders are increasingly positioned on the long side."

Instead of:
"Positioning is becoming heavier."

Prefer:
"Traders are adding exposure."
"More leverage is entering the market."
"Outstanding positions continue to build."

The goal is natural writing, not simply different wording.

4. Distinguish observation from implication.

Avoid making the observation and implication say the same thing.

For example:

Observation:
"Coinbase and Kraken prices are diverging."

Implication:
"Wider price differences leave more room for cross-exchange arbitrage."

The implication should answer "Why does this matter?" rather than restating the observation.

5. Avoid unnecessary repetition.

The headline already contains the key event.

Do not immediately repeat the same wording in the body.

Each sentence should add context, explanation, or significance.

6. Prefer descriptive statements over predictions.

Because these posts are generated automatically, prioritize statements directly supported by the data.

Prefer:

Positioning is becoming more one-sided.
Long leverage was reduced.
Funding has shifted below neutral.
Futures are trading at a premium to spot.

Avoid predicting future price direction or market behavior unless the data itself directly supports the statement.

7. Keep numerical information out of headlines when it already appears immediately below.

Example:

Instead of:

"BTC funding turns negative at -0.0120%"

Prefer:

"BTC funding turns negative"

The data section already contains the precise values.

8. Include a clear Rarity/Context line whenever the data supports it (e.g. "Sharpest compression in 21 days", "Largest move in 90 days", "New all-time high in OI").

9. Final quality check.

Before accepting a template, ask:

Does every sentence add new information?
Does the wording sound like a human market analyst?
Is the implication factual instead of predictive?
Is there any unnecessary repetition?
Would an experienced trader naturally write this?
